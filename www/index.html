Class info

Time: Tuesday 7:20-9:45PM

Location: C1-308

This 11-week seminar course is geared towards first or second-year graduate students who are interested in the fundamental aspects and the state-of-art developments of machine learning techniques. In this semester, we will cover a number of representation learning topics, including non-linear dimensionality reduction, metric learning, generative modeling, transformer-based models and deep graph representation learning.

Prerequisites: Students who have taken a graduate-level introductory machine learning class (e.g. Learning from Data or its equivalent) and received a final grade of B or above. Sufficient background in math, probability and optimization for understanding research papers is also expected.

For more information about the class policies, see the class syllabus.
